Notice
Two sentences hiding inside one

Watch London do it: “…he paused for breath at the top, excusing the act to himself by looking at his watch.” There are three actions in that sentence — pausing, excusing, looking — but only one clause. The other two actions travel as verbals: verb forms demoted from running the sentence to working inside it. That's compression, and it's one of the most powerful moves a sentence writer owns.

The before/after is dramatic. Uncompressed: The fans screamed the fight song. The fans stormed the court. Compressed: Screaming the fight song, the fans stormed the court. Same facts, one sentence, simultaneous motion. Tour the three verbal types, then the phrases they grow into.

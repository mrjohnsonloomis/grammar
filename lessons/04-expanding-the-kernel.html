Notice
How professionals hang detail on a kernel

Hemingway's famous opening is a modest kernel — we lived — wearing five prepositional phrases: In the late summer of that year (when) in a house (where) in a village (where) across the river and the plain (where) to the mountains (where). Douglass stacks place-phrases like a surveyor: in Tuckahoe, near Hillsborough, about twelve miles from Easton, in Talbot county, Maryland. The kernel states; the phrases situate.

A phrase is any word group that works as a unit but has no subject–verb pair — that one test separates phrases from the clauses you'll meet in Lesson 06. Watch Hemingway's opening grow from its two-word kernel, then tour the workhorse expansion phrases and build with them.

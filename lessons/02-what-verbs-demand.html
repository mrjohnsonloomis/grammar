Notice
Verbs come with demands

He slept is finished. She grabbed is not — grabbed what? Some verbs complete the move alone; others hand their action off and feel broken until something receives it. Linking verbs make a different demand: The gym smelled leaves a promise hanging until a description arrives.

Frederick Douglass ends the first paragraph of his Narrative on this machinery: “…to keep their slaves thus ignorant.” The verb keep demands both a receiver (their slaves) and a completer (ignorant) — and Douglass parks the completer at the very end of the paragraph, where it rings. Knowing a verb's demands tells you what your sentence still owes — and where its weight will land.

Notice
Reading like a writer

You already build with subjects, verbs, objects, and completions. This lesson opens the whole toolbox: the eight parts of speech, every kind of word English gives you to build with. The rule that runs through everything: a word's part of speech is the job it's doing in its sentence — run is a verb in I run and a noun in a run; the little word well can be an adverb, an adjective, or a sigh.

Below are passages from five great writers. Pick a category to light up its words, then click any word to see two things: the job it's doing, and why the author might have chosen it. This isn't dissection — it's shopping for moves.

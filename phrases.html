Phrases

A phrase is a group of words that acts as a unit — but it has no subject-verb pair of its own. Click through each type to see how phrases work inside real sentences.

First, a Quick Distinction
Before we dive in: every multi-word chunk in English is either a phrase or a clause. The difference is one thing.

Phrase

A group of words that works as a single unit but does NOT contain a subject + verb pair.

on the old wooden porch No subject. No verb. A unit modifying something else.

Clause

A group of words that DOES contain a subject + verb pair — a mini-sentence inside a sentence.

because the rain was falling Subject: the rain. Verb: was falling.
Phrase = no S+V  ·  Clause = has S+V  ·  That single test decides everything.

Verbals

Verbals are verb forms in disguise — they look like verbs but do the work of nouns, adjectives, or adverbs. Learn the three types, see them build into phrases, then practice spotting them in the wild.

What Is a Verbal?

A verbal is a word formed from a verb that does not function as a verb in the sentence. It borrows the verb's shape but lends its meaning to some other part of speech — noun, adjective, or adverb.

Because verbals look like verbs, they're easy to misidentify. The test is never the word's form — it's the job the word is doing in its sentence.

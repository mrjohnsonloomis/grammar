Writers get stuck when they picture no reader, or an impossible one — a stern grader who already knows everything and is hunting for mistakes. The fix is to picture a real reader and write to them. Once you know who you’re writing for, a hundred small decisions answer themselves: how much to explain, where to slow down, what to assume.

Audience works a little differently in the two kinds of writing we do, so this splits into two short pages:

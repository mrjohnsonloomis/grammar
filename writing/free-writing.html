By putting a lot of “low-stakes” writing into our practice — ungraded, and often seen by no one but you — we get to use writing as a way of thinking. As a bonus, we build a written record of our thoughts. You’ll know what you were thinking when you thought it, and that makes the high-stakes writing (essays) far easier later. I promise.

The rules that make it work

Keep the pen moving

Try your best not to lift the pen the whole time. Even if you’re stuck, write about being stuck. Thinking begets thinking, so write through the blocks. No excuses — a moving pen is the entire technique.

You choose what to share

You’ll sometimes share writing — but never all of it, and never without warning. You can bracket the part you’re willing to share (a paragraph, a sentence, a phrase), so you always keep control of what leaves the page.

One notebook, all term

Keep a single notebook that is just for English, and bring it every day. The record only works if it’s all in one place — and carrying it is part of treating writing as a daily practice.
